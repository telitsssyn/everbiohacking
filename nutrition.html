Nutrition

Biohacker’s Fuel: The Ultimate Guide to Precision Nutrition

Nutrition is the most powerful tool in a biohacker's arsenal. To optimize cognitive function, longevity, and physical performance, we move away from "dieting" and toward nutrient density and metabolic efficiency.

1. The Core Philosophy

The goal is simple: maximize micronutrients while minimizing systemic inflammation and glucose spikes.

  • Whole Food Focus: Prioritize vegetables, fruits, berries, legumes, nuts, and wild-caught seafood.
  • The "Clean Slate" Rule: Eliminate added sugars, excessive salt, and processed condiments like commercial ketchups or mayonnaises.
  • Metabolic Rhythm: Maintain consistent energy levels with meals every 3–4 hours to avoid cortisol spikes.
  • Balance is Key: Every plate should be a synergy of complex carbohydrates, high-quality proteins, and essential fats.
  • Mindful Consumption: Avoid overeating. Aim for 2600 kcal (depending on your activity level) and stop just before you feel full.

2. High-Performance Macronutrients

Healthy Fats

Fats are the building blocks of your brain and hormones.

  • Coconut oil, Ghee, olive oil, flaxseed oil, peanut butter, butter, cream, whole milk, nuts, avocado, seeds (chia, flax).

Bioavailable Proteins

Essential for muscle repair and neurotransmitter synthesis.

  • Eggs, cottage cheese, greek yogurt, salmon, tuna, mackerel, trout, shrimp, squid rings, beef, chicken, lamb, horse meat, rabbit, caviar, mushrooms, parmesan, gouda, legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans, peas).

Complex Carbohydrates & Fiber

Fuel for your gut microbiome and brain.

  • Oatmeal, buckwheat, rice, quinoa, whole-grain bread, lavash, tortillas, berries, fruits, and vegetables.

3. Berries, Fruits, and Vegetables

Focus on the Glycemic Index. Berries are the "gold standard" for biohackers due to high antioxidants and low sugar.

  • Berries: Bilberries, blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, blackberries.
  • Fruits: Bananas, oranges, avocado, persimmon, kiwi, peaches, apricots, pineapple, apples, pears, lemons.
  • Dried Fruits: Dried apricots, prunes, dates, raisins, figs, dried persimmon. Monitor portion sizes due to high sugar concentration.
  • Vegetables: Carrots, asparagus, tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, garlic, bell peppers, beets, cabbage.

4. Hydration & Liquid Optimization

  • The Foundation: 1–2 liters of pure water + mineral water for electrolyte balance.
  • Functional Drinks: Green tea, rosehip tea, ginger tea.
  • Cognitive Boosters: High-quality coffee and cacao (rich in polyphenols).
  • Natural Elixirs: Freshly squeezed juices, fruit waters (mors), compotes.
